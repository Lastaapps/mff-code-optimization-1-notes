## Data-Flow Analysis Example (CFG)
A diagram illustrating constant propagation through a simple IF-ELSE structure:

1. **Top level**: `a = UND` (Undefined)
2. **IF statement**:
    - **Left Branch**: `a = 0`
    - **Right Branch**: `a = 1`
3. **Merging**: The branches merge before reaching block 4.
4. **Block 4**: `b = a`

**Lattice state evolution**:
- At entry of IF: `a = UND`
- Exit of Left Branch: `a = 0`
- Exit of Right Branch: `a = 1`
- After merge: `a = VAR` (Variable/Unknown constant) because `0 ∧ 1 = VAR`.

## Meet (Join Operation) ∧
The meet operation defines how values from different paths are combined:
- `0 ∧ 0 = 0`
- `0 ∧ 1 = VAR`
- `VAR ∧ * = VAR`

**Data-Flow Analysis**
- (Gary Kildall, 1973)
- CPM 70's - 80's
- **Semi-Lattice** of values to propagate (referred to as "Polosvaz" in Czech context).
