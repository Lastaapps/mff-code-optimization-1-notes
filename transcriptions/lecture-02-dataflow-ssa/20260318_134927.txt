## CFG to Dominator Tree

### CFG Diagram (Left)
- **ENTRY**
- **Block A**: Successors B and C.
- **Block B**: Successor D.
- **Block C**: Successor D.
- **Block D**: Successor EXIT and back-edge to A.
- **EXIT**

### Dominator Tree (Right)
The dominator tree represents the immediate dominance relationships.

- **ENTRY** (0, 11)
  - **A** (1, 10)
    - **B** (2, 3)
    - **C** (4, 5)
    - **D** (6, 9)
      - **EXIT** (7, 8)

#### DFS Traversal Numbers
The pairs $(in, out)$ next to each node indicate the entry and exit times during a Depth-First Search (DFS) of the dominator tree:
- `A` is the immediate dominator of `B`, `C`, and `D`.
- `D` is the immediate dominator of `EXIT`.
- Note: Although `D` has predecessors `B` and `C` in the CFG, its immediate dominator is `A` because `A` is the last node common to all paths reaching `D`.
