## Partial Redundancy Elimination (PRE)

### Concept
PRE is a powerful optimization that combines Common Subexpression Elimination (CSE) and Loop Invariant Code Motion (LICM). It eliminates expressions that are redundant on some, but not necessarily all, paths.

### Diagram
The blackboard shows a CFG where:
1. An expression `A + B` is computed in two different branches.
2. In a successor block where these branches merge, `A + B` is computed again.
3. This third computation is **redundant** because it was already computed on any path reaching this block.

- Mention of "98" (likely referring to the year of a significant paper or GCC implementation update).

---

## Transformation Sketch
- The redundant expression `A + B` in the bottom block is crossed out.
- The goal of PRE is to move such computations to the earliest possible point (anticipability) where they are still useful.
