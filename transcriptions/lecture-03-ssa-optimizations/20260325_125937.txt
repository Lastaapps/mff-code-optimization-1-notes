## Loop Invariant Code Motion (LICM)
The blackboard shows a CFG with a loop and how an expression `A+B` can be moved.

- **Loop Structure**: A header block leads to a body containing `A+B`, which then loops back or exits.
- **Hoisting**: The expression `A+B` is moved from the loop body to a **pre-header** block.
- **Visuals**:
  - Red arrow: Control flow path through the loop.
  - Yellow arrow: Indicates the hoisting of `A+B` out of the loop.
  - This is only safe if `A+B` is loop-invariant (operands $A$ and $B$ are not modified within the loop).
