## Down-Safety
**Definition:** Expression $E$ is **down-safe** in Basic Block $B$ iff $E$ is calculated on every path from $B$ (before any operand is redefined).

## Anticipancy (Backward Analysis)
$$ANT IN(B) = EUSE(B) \cup (ANT OUT(B) \setminus KILL(B))$$
$$ANT OUT(B) = \bigcap_{S \in SUCC(B)} ANT IN(S)$$

---

## CFG Diagram
Diagram illustrating the flow of anticipancy:
- A block $B$ has multiple successors $S_1, S_2, \dots$
- `ANT OUT(B)` is the intersection of `ANT IN` of its successors.
- If `A+B` is calculated in all paths starting from $B$, it is "anticipated" at the end of $B$.
