## Factored Use-Def (FUD) Chains
- A diagram shows a central node connecting multiple definitions ($a_1, a_2, \dots$) to a single use.
- Labeled as **FUD (Factored Use-Def)** or **Factored SSA**. This was a precursor/alternative to standard SSA form used in some compilers (like IBM XL or early GCC).

## Common Subexpression Elimination (Refined)
### Partially Available Expressions (After Busy Code Motion)
**Forward Analysis**

$$AV IN(B) = \bigcap_{P \in PRED(B)} AV OUT(P)$$

$$AV OUT(B) = ANT IN(B) \cup (AV IN(B) \setminus KILL(B))$$
*(Note: In the second equation, $EUSE(B)$ was crossed out and replaced with $ANT IN(B)$, reflecting the context of PRE/LCM where anticipancy defines potential availability)*
