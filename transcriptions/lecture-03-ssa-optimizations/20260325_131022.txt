## DFS and Backedges in CFG
The blackboard shows a CFG being analyzed using Depth First Search (DFS) to identify loop structures.

- **DFS BACKEDGE**: An edge from a node to one of its ancestors in the DFS tree. This is the formal way to identify loops in a reducible CFG.
- **Diagram Visuals**:
  - Entry block (top)
  - A loop header.
  - A block containing `A/B`.
  - An exit block.
  - Circular arrows indicate the backedge forming the loop.
  - Yellow/orange lines trace the DFS traversal paths.
